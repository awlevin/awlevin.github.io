About Me

I have an ambitious dream of bringing more equal opportunity to this world. Not everyone has had the same privilege as I've had to learn and pursue their dreams. So I've pondered over how I can leave the biggest impact on society within my lifetime. The most obvious and exciting solution is computers. Through software, I have unlimited creative potential, and I can deploy my ideas across the world in a matter of seconds.

The availability of knowledge and education is essential for people to pursue their dreams. One of my favorite online resources is Wikipedia. Unlimited information, free and easily obtainable, and anyone with internet can use it. The next step in making information more available is through Artificial Intelligence, and I want to be at the forefront of that revolution.

Discovering Computer Science Textbook

Student Technical Editor • May 2015 - August 2015

Worked on a team of 6 undergraduates to drastically improve the style, pedagogy, and concisision of an open-sourced introductory computer engineering textbook. Traditional intro computer engineering textbooks are written from a "bottom-up" approach (teaching hardware before software), so the goal of this text was to see if a less conventional "top-down" approach was more effective. The text was later used in a course taught by Professor Karu Sankaralingam, whom I worked closely with throughout the revision process.

Involvement

National Organization for Business and Engineering (NOBE)

Technology Director • National Outreach Representative• August 2015 - Present

Just before I began attending UW-Madison, NOBE was a failing student organization that eventually disbanded my freshmen year. When a roommate of mine asked me if I'd be interested joining, I immediately got on-board. Myself and 3 others interviewed over 50 highly qualified applicants, and selected only 10 to form our new Executive Board.

As Technology Director, I was responsible for website and online calendar maintenance, general bookkeeping, and arranging new platforms for efficient communication. As National Outreach Representative, it was my responsibility to communicate important deadlines, goals, and accomplishments between the NOBE National Board and our individual chapter.
Click here to see our website!

BadgerLoop (Elon Musk HyperLoop Competition)

Software Team Member • January 2016 - April 2016

The software team developed an online dashboard and a mobile app for the UW HyperLoop Pod. The online dashboard is crucial to the success and safety of the pod, as it retrieves and displays data from every component of the pod. If a component is malfunctioning, the dashboard will intelligently detect the malfunction and bring it to prominence for the user, also providing an emergency "STOP" button that cues the pod's signature "Eddy Current" brakes.
Check out the BadgerLoop website here! Or check out the mobile app!

Zeta Beta Tau – Alpha Kappa Chapter

Pledge Class President • General Member • January 2015 - January 2016

As Spring 2015 pledge class presdient, I was responsible for communicating all important information and deadlines between the executive board and my 8 person pledge class. I was personally held accountable for any misscomunications or missed deadlines. As a team, we worked tirelessly for a semester to learn all fraternal history and traditions. Due to personal disagreements with Greek Life as a whole, and my desire to diversify myself, I ultimately parted ways with ZBT after one semester of being recogznied as a fully initiated brother.
